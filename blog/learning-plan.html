10 November 2010

Long term goal or career pathway?

Technology in general is exciting to me. I'm starting off with the front end but I would like to also learn back end and system architecture. At the end of the bootcamp I intend to look for a job that will allow me to flex my skills and grow further into other areas of the technology stack. I'm keeping my expectations open here as I want to be open to taking whatever exciting opportunities come my way.

At first I will be looking for a junior/intermediate role where I can get a lot of experience in working with FE/BE. I also have some ideas on apps that I would like to work on. Joining an open source project to assist and learn would be a good way to start. Later on I would like to develop my own app ideas to solve a problems I come across where I am by which I mean I want to start local.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Staying focussed is going to be the key and I have taken steps to make sure I remind myself of what my goals are here every day. I'm going to be tempted at times by other side projects that I have and want to spend time on those. When that happens will just need to gently nudge myself back on course and remember that I will have time for those other projects at the end.

My strengths and limitations concerning learning

My comprehension is strong so I typically pick up concepts and ideas quickly. I struggle with rote learning so reference material is important for me. I'm good at finding references and remembering where to look for information. I can be distracted a bit too easily and sometimes I struggle to stay engaged.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would I like to develop while at Dev Academy?

I'm going to focus on attention control and reaching out for help early instead of trying to figure out and fix everything myself. I struggle with feeling inadequate when I am unable to solve something on my own. I would also like to further develop my leadership abilities.

Workload management

Managing myself to be a good productive collaborator

  • Maintain a healthy sleep schedule
  • Remember to move daily
  • Check in daily and stay active on discord.
  • Be responsive to help and feedback requests.
  • Be respectful of others experiences and journey.

What will I do when things are building up and starting to get to me?

This is a time when I will need to step back from what I'm working on. Play with the dogs or go for a walk. Just take a breather and then come back and try again. If there is any risk of missing deadlines I will reach out to a facilitator and any team members affected to let them know so we can get on top of it early. I will do this as soon as it become apparent to me that I am.

A commitment as to how and where I will seek help in a timely way

Expectations of self during Bootcamp

I will reach out on discord to either my immediate team/facilitator or public channels if I am stuck. Remembering to try and solve myself first for between 15 and 30min. I will first seek assistance from my peers and then my facilitators.

What I expect from the Bootcamp team

Honesty and candid feedback I the primary things I expect here. Feedback and improvement recommendations on any work I submit. I would like to be challenged where possible. I benefit from a bit of pushing from any sort of coach and so I'm really hoping that I will get nudged to stretch my abilities.

I would also appreciate 1-to-1 feedback on how I'm doing and a check in regularly.

External scheduling demands.

I play football on the weekends and this includes 2 trainings a week. I will avoid having any commitments during these times.

  • Tuesday 6pm - 9pm
