Reflections on foundations core content

These are my answers to the reflection questions of the core content covered with the 5 week foundations course for Dev Academy. I've enjoyed these 5 weeks of material and I'm feeling ready for bootcamp to start. I'm going to spend the week catching up on things I won't have time for in the 9 weeks intensive portion of the course and keeping the last 5 weeks of learning fresh in my mind.

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

This is a hard question for me to answer but after some reflection this is my list:

  • I'm percieved by some to lack confidence in my own abilities and that they might be right.
  • Overcoming my mental block for writing reflections is the key for me.
  • I can get good at this.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values are your guiding light. They help you stay pointed in the right direction. Keeping your values in mind when you set out on a journey and regardless of where you end up you will know that you have walked the right path. Journey before destination.

Empathy is what allows us to connect with ourselves and others emotionally. We as humans are emotional creatures and being in touch with your own emotions and the emotions of those around you facilitates good learning and collaboration environments. Where we can be open and honest in achieving our goals.

Self-awareness is a key component of almost everything anyone does to improve themselves. Regardless of your own personal traits and foibles being aware of them allows you to strengthen yourself. You can play to your strengths and shore up your weaknesses. I believe all journeys benefit from self awareness.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I was surprised by how much of it was revision for me. I didn't realise how much of these concepts I had already covered previously. It was refreshing to see the content covered in a comprehensive manner that felt very accessible and I enjoyed engaging with the material.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

While I'm happy to read and think about core learning competancies I have a block when it comes to writing about these things. I found it hard to get over my procrastination hump and put words down for my blogs.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think it's because programming is a very collaborative thing. There are very few projects where you can work alone. Software is becoming more and more complex and as complexity and scale goes up so does the need to for collaboration. Not only that but you often need to collaborate with designers, clients, and managers to deliver a project. All this interaction means that being good at human interaction is an invaluable tool for the professional programmer.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No it doesn't feel like a waste. Even though I have covered this material in previous courses there is always more to learn and develop about yourself. I believe collaboration is the key to achieving anything significance and I would like to achieve some things of significance.
